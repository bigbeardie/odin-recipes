Pork Gyozas

Description

Gyoza are a Japanese pan-fried dumpling made with a delicate wrapper and filled with seasoned vegetables and meat (or just vegetable) filling

Ingredients

  • 150g Chinese cabbage, finely chopped
  • 50g Bamboo shoots, finely chopped
  • 450g Minced pork
  • 1 Tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon of mirin
  • 2 Tablespoons of finely chopped shallots
  • 3cm piece of ginger root, peeled and grated
  • 1 Tablespoon of sesame seeds, briefly in a hot dry frying pan
  • 1 Tablespoon of roughly chopped coriander
  • 1/2 Teaspoon sugar
  • 1 Egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 Red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
  • Salt and white pepper
  • 80 Wonton skins
  • Vegetable oil
  • Gyoza sauce

Steps

  1. Put all the ingredients into a bowl and mix together. Thoroughly season with salt and pepper
  2. Put a teaspoon of the mixture in the centre of each wonton skin
  3. Moisten the edges with a little water and then fold over to make a half-moon shape. Press down to form a seal
  4. Heat a large frying pan over a medium heat for 1 to 2 minutes until hot and almost smoking and add a tablespoon of vegetable oil
  5. Reduce the heat to moderate
  6. Put 4-6 dumplings in the pan and saute gently for 1 minute each side or until starting to brown
  7. Don't overcrowd the pan or they will stew
  8. Remove the pan from the heat, add 3 tablespoons of water and cover immediatley with a lid or foil
  9. Return to the heat for 1 minute, then remove and set aside for a further 2 minutes, by which time the gyozas will be heated through
  10. Repeat for the remaining gyozas
